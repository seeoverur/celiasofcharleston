Small & Independent Groceries

North Charleston & West Ashley

Beyond the big chains, these are the small neighborhood markets, corner stores, and specialty grocers worth knowing on both sides of the Ashley River.

North Charleston

Neighborhood Markets

North Charleston's small grocers lean local and international, filling in the gaps left by the big-box stores with produce, specialty ingredients, and neighborhood staples.

Fresh Future Farm

Chicora's community-grown grocery store

A nonprofit neighborhood grocery store in the Chicora neighborhood that sells produce grown on-site along with eggs, milk, and pantry staples, priced on a sliding scale to keep them accessible.

Neighborhood
Chicora
Best For
Locally-grown produce

El Molino Supermarket

A full-service Latin American grocer

Set up like a traditional grocery store with a butcher and seafood counter on top of aisles of Latin American products, plus a small restaurant counter serving authentic dishes on site.

Neighborhood
North Charleston
Best For
Latin American groceries & butcher counter

H&L Asian Market

Rivers Avenue's spot for Asian pantry staples

A reliable stop on Rivers Avenue for Asian ingredients, sauces, produce, and pantry goods that are harder to find at the mainstream grocery chains nearby.

Neighborhood
Rivers Avenue
Best For
Asian ingredients & sauces

La Tienda Latina

Dorchester Road's Latin American specialty grocer

A go-to for Latin American specialty items on Dorchester Road, from fresh produce and spices to imported pantry staples that round out a home-cooked meal.

Neighborhood
Dorchester Road
Best For
Latin American specialty items

St. Mark's Best Little Store

A Park Circle staple for quick grabs

A neighborhood staple on O'Hear Avenue for Park Circle residents making smaller, more frequent runs rather than one big weekly shop.

Neighborhood
Park Circle
Best For
Quick neighborhood grabs
West Ashley

Local & Seasonal Picks

West Ashley's grocery scene is dominated by the larger chains, so the best "small" options here tend to be seasonal and market-based rather than storefronts. Here's the standout.

West Ashley Farmers Market

A weekly community market at Ackerman Park

Running Wednesdays from April through October, this community market brings together local farmers and small producers for fresh produce, meats, baked goods, and prepared foods in a relaxed, family-friendly setting.

Neighborhood
Ackerman Park, West Ashley
Best For
Fresh, local, and seasonal goods
